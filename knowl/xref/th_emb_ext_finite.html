Theorem 1.10.11. Extensions of embeddings: finite extension.

Let \(K/F\) be a field extension of degree \(n\text{.}\) Given a field embedding \(\sigma\colon F\rightarrow L\text{,}\) there are at most \(n\) extensions of \(\sigma\) to \(K\text{.}\)

Proof.

The proof is by strong induction on \(n=[K\colon F]\text{.}\)
Base step: \(n=1\text{.}\) In this case \(K=F\text{,}\) and the only extension of \(\sigma\) to \(K\) is \(\sigma\) itself. Thus there are in fact exactly \(n=1\) extensions in this case.
Strong induction step: let \(n> 1\) and assume the result holds for all field extensions of degree \(k\) with \(k< n\text{.}\) Since \(n\geq 2\text{,}\) we can find \(\alpha\in K-F\text{,}\) yielding a tower of extensions \(F\leq F(\alpha)\leq K\text{.}\) Since
\begin{equation*} [K\colon F(\alpha)][F(\alpha)\colon F]=[K\colon F]=n\text{,} \end{equation*}
and \([F(\alpha)\colon F]=d> 1\text{,}\) we have \(k=[K\colon F(\alpha)]< n\text{.}\)
Making use of the notation in Lemma 1.10.9, let \(X\) be the set of extensions of \(\sigma\) to \(K\text{,}\) let \(X'\) be the set of extensions of \(\sigma\) to \(K'=F(\alpha)\text{,}\) and for each \(\tau'\in X'\text{,}\) let \(X_{\tau'}\) be the set of all extensions of \(\tau'\) to \(K\text{.}\) Since \(K'=F(\alpha)\) is simple, Theorem 1.10.11 implies that
\begin{equation*} \abs{X'}\leq [K'\colon F]=d\text{.} \end{equation*}
Next using the induction hypothesis on the extension \(K/K'\) of degree \([K\colon K']=k < n\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} \abs{X_{\tau'}}\leq [K\colon K']=k \end{equation*}
for each \(\tau'\in X'\text{.}\) Finally using (1.22), we conclude that
\begin{align*} \abs{X} \amp =\sum_{\tau'\in X'}\abs{X_{\tau'}}\\ \amp \leq \sum_{\tau'\in X'}k\\ \amp = k\abs{X'}\\ \amp \leq kd\\ \amp = n\text{,} \end{align*}
as desired.
