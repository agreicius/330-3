Theorem 1.21.8. Cubic resolvent.

Let \(f(x)=x^4+ax^3+bx^2+cx+d\in F[x]\) be a degree-4 polynomial, and let \(R_f(x)\) be its cubic resolvent.
  1. \(R_f(x)\in F[x]\text{.}\)
  2. \(R_f(x)=x^3-bx^2+(ac-4d)x-(a^2d+c^2-4bd)\text{.}\) In particular, we have the special cases
    \begin{align} f(x)=x^4+cx+d \amp \implies R_f(x)=x^3-4dx-c^2\tag{1.38}\\ f(x)=x^4+bx^2+d \amp \implies R_f(x)=x^3-bx^2-4d+4bd\text{.}\tag{1.39} \end{align}
  3. The discriminant of \(R_f(x)\) is equal to the discriminant of \(f(x)\text{.}\) In particular, \(R_f(x)\) has no repeated roots if and only if \(f(x)\) has no repeated roots.

Proof.

Statement (1) follows from (2), which can be proved using symmetric polynomial techniques that are outside the scope of this course. Statement (3) follows from the definition of the discriminant, and is left as an exercise.
