Corollary 1.9.9. Uniqueness of finite fields.

Fix a positive integer \(n\text{.}\) Any two fields of cardinality \(p^n\) are isomorphic. In particular, given any two irreducible polynomials \(f,g\in \F_p[x]\) of degree \(n\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} \F_p[x]/(f)\cong \F_p[x]/(g)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Proof.

Fix a positive integer \(n\text{.}\) Let \(\Fpbar/\F_p\) and let \(K/\F_p\) be the unique subextension of \(\Fpbar/\F_p\) of cardinality \(p^n\text{.}\) Given any other field \(K'\) of cardinality \(p^n\text{,}\) we must have \(\character K'=p\text{,}\) and hence \(\F_p\leq K'\) is a finite extension. By Theorem 1.9.7, \(K'=\F_p(\alpha)\) for some \(\alpha\in K'\text{,}\) and thus \(K'\cong \F_p[x]/(f)\) for some irreducible polynomial \(f\in \F_p[x]\) of degree \(n\text{.}\) Let \(\alpha\in \Fpbar\) be a root of \(f\text{,}\) and let
\begin{align*} \phi\colon \F_p[x]/(f(x)) \amp \Fpbar \\ \overline{h}(x)\amp\mapsto h(\alpha) \text{.} \end{align*}
By Theorem 1.4.7, \(\phi\) is a ring homomorphism. It follows that \(K'\cong\phi(K')\leq \Fpbar\text{,}\) Since \(\abs{\phi(K')}=p^n\text{,}\) we must have \(\phi(K')=K\text{,}\) by the uniqueness claim in Theorem 1.9.1. This proves that any field of cardinality \(p^n\) is isomorphic to the unique subfield of \(\Fpbar/\F_p\) of cardinality \(p^n\text{;}\) we conclude by transitivity that any two such fields are isomorphic.
