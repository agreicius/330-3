Fiat 1.1.1. Rings have identity.

In this course all rings \(R\) are assumed to have a multiplicative identity element. By default the additive identity element of \(R\) is denoted \(0\) (or \(0_R\)), and the multiplicative identity element is denoted \(1\) (or \(1_R\)).
Furthermore, our definition of a ring homomorphism \(\phi\colon R\rightarrow S\) includes the condition that \(\phi(1_R)=1_S\text{:}\) i.e., by definition a ring homomorphism must map the multiplicative identity to the multiplicative identity.
