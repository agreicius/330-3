Theorem 1.4.7. Quotients of polynomial rings.

Let \(F\) be a field and let \(f(x)=\anpoly\) be a nonconstant polynomial in \(F[x]\text{.}\) Following NotationÂ 1.4.5, given \(g\in F[x]\text{,}\) we denote by \(\overline{g}\) its image in \(F[x]/(f)\) under the quotient map.
  1. Simply generated.
    We have \(F[x]/(f(x))=F[\overline{x}]\text{:}\) i.e., \(F[x]/(f)\) is generated over \(F\) as a ring by \(\overline{x}\text{,}\) the image of \(x\) under the quotient map.
  2. Dimension.
    Let \(n=\deg f\text{.}\) The set \(B=\{1,\overline{x},\dots, \overline{x}^{n-1}\}\) is a basis of \(F[x]/(f)\) as a vector space over \(F\text{,}\) and thus \(\dim_F F[x]/(f)=\abs{B}=n\text{.}\)
  3. Mapping property.
    Let \(S\) be a nonzero ring, and let \(\sigma\colon F\rightarrow S\) be a ring homomorphism. Given any \(s\in S\) satisfying \(f^\sigma(s)=0\text{,}\) where
    \begin{equation*} f^\sigma=\sigma(a_n)x^n+\sigma(a_{n-1})x^{n-1}+\cdots +\sigma(a_1)x+\sigma(a_0)\text{,} \end{equation*}
    the map
    \begin{align*} \tau\colon F[x]/(f) \amp \rightarrow S\\ b_m\overline{x}^m+b_{m-1}\overline{x}^{m-1}+\cdots +b_1\overline{x}+b_0 \amp\longmapsto \sigma(b_m)s^m+\sigma(b_{m-1})s^{m-1}+\cdots +\sigma(b_1)s+\sigma(b_0) \end{align*}
    is a ring homomorphism. Furthermore, the map
    \begin{equation*} \tau\mapsto \tau(\overline{x}) \end{equation*}
    is a bijection between the set of all ring homomorphisms \(\tau\colon F[x]/(f)\rightarrow S\) satisfying \(\tau\vert_F=\sigma\) and the set of elements of \(s\in S\) satisfying \(f^\sigma(s)=0\text{.}\)
    In other words, a ring homomorphism from \(F[x]/(f)\) to \(S\) extending \(\sigma\colon F\rightarrow S\) is uniquely determined by where it sends \(\overline{x}\text{,}\) and this image must be a root of the polynomial \(f^\sigma\text{.}\)

Proof.

The proof of this result relies on various bits of theory from general ring theory. It is left as an (excellent) exercises.
