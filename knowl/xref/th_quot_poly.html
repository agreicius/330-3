Theorem 1.4.7. Quotients of polynomial rings.

Let \(F\) be a field and let \(f(x)=\anpoly\) be a nonconstant polynomial in \(F[x]\text{.}\) Following NotationÂ 1.4.5, given \(g\in F[x]\text{,}\) we denote by \(\overline{g}\) its image in \(F[x]/(f)\) under the quotient map.
  1. Simply generated.
    We have \(F[x]/(f(x))=F[\overline{x}]\text{:}\) i.e., \(F[x]/(f)\) is generated over \(F\) as a ring by \(\overline{x}\text{,}\) the image of \(x\) under the quotient map.
  2. Dimension.
    Let \(n=\deg f\text{.}\) The set \(B=\{1,\overline{x},\dots, \overline{x}^{n-1}\}\) is a basis of \(F[x]/(f)\) as a vector space over \(F\text{,}\) and thus \(\dim_F F[x]/(f)=\abs{B}=n\text{.}\)
  3. Mapping property.
    Let \(S\) be a nonzero ring. The set of all ring homomorphisms \(\overline{\phi}\colon F[x]/(f)\rightarrow S\) is in bijective correspondence with pairs \((\phi, s)\) where \(\phi\colon F\rightarrow S\) is a ring homomorphism and \(s\in S\) is a root of
    \begin{equation*} \phi(f)=\phi(a_n)x^n+\phi(a_{n-1})x^{n-1}+\cdots +\phi(a_1)x+\phi(a_0)\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Indeed, given such a pair \((\phi, s)\text{,}\) we define
    \begin{align*} \overline{\phi}\colon F[x]/(f) \amp \longrightarrow S\\ b_m\overline{x}^m+b_{m-1}\overline{x}^{m-1}+\cdots +b_1\overline{x}+b_0 \amp\longmapsto \phi(b_m)s^m+\phi(b_{m-1})s^{m-1}+\cdots +\phi(b_1)s+\phi(b_0) \text{.} \end{align*}

Proof.

The proof of this result relies on various bits of theory from general ring theory. It is left as an (excellent) exercises.
