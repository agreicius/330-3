Theorem 1.10.3. Extensions of embeddings: simple extension.

Let \(F(\alpha)\) be a simple algebraic extension of degree \(n\text{,}\) and let \(f(x)=m_{\alpha,F}(x)\text{.}\) Given a field embedding \(\sigma\colon F\rightarrow K\text{,}\) define
\begin{equation*} X=\{\tau\colon F(\alpha)\rightarrow K\ \colon \tau \text{ an embedding}, \tau\vert_F=\sigma\}\text{,} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} R=\{\beta\in K\colon f^\sigma(\beta)=0\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
In other words, \(X\) is the set of all extensions of \(\sigma\) to \(F(\alpha)\text{,}\) and \(R\) is the set of all roots of \(f^\sigma(x)\) in \(K\text{.}\)
  1. Given any \(\beta\in R\text{,}\) the map \(\tau\colon F(\alpha)\rightarrow L\) defined as
    \begin{equation} \tau(a_0+a_1\alpha+\cdots +a_{n-1}\alpha^{n-1})=\sigma(a_0)+\sigma(a_1)\beta+\cdots +\sigma(a_{n-1})\beta^{n-1}\tag{1.19} \end{equation}
    is an extension of \(\sigma\) to \(F(\alpha)\text{.}\)
  2. The map
    \begin{align*} X \amp \rightarrow R \\ \tau \amp \mapsto \tau(\alpha) \end{align*}
    defines a bijection between \(X\) and \(R\text{.}\) In other words, the distinct extensions of \(\sigma\) to \(F(\alpha)\) are in bijective correspondence with the roots of \(f^\sigma(x)\) in \(K\text{.}\)
  3. We have \(\abs{X}\leq n=\deg_F\alpha\text{.}\) In other words, there are at most \(n\) extensions of \(\sigma\) to \(F(\alpha)\text{.}\)
