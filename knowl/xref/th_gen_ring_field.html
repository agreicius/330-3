Theorem 1.3.4. Generated ring versus generated field.

Let \(K/F\) be a field extension, and let \(\alpha\in K\text{.}\) Recall that \(F[\alpha]\) denotes the subring of \(K\) generated by \(\alpha\) over \(F\text{.}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle F[\alpha]\leq F(\alpha)\)
  2. \(F[\alpha]=F(\alpha)\) if and only if \(F[\alpha]\) is a field.

Proof.

  1. By definition \(F[\alpha]\) is the intersection of all subrings containing \(F\) and \(\alpha\text{,}\) and thus is a subset of \(F(\alpha)\) which is, the intersection of all subfields containing \(F\) and \(\alpha\text{.}\)
  2. Clearly, if \(F[\alpha]=F(\alpha)\text{,}\) then \(F[\alpha]\) is a field. Assume on the other hand that \(F[\alpha]\) is a field. Since \(F[\alpha]\) is a field containing \(F\) and \(\alpha\text{,}\) and since \(F(\alpha)\) is the smallest such field, we have \(F(\alpha)\leq F[\alpha]\text{.}\) But by (1) we always have \(F[\alpha]\leq F(\alpha)\text{.}\) Thus \(F[\alpha]=F(\alpha)\text{.}\)
