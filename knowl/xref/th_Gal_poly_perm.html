Theorem 1.20.3. Galois group as permutation group.

Let \(f(x)\in F[x]\) be a separable irreducible polynomial of degree \(n\text{,}\) let \(\Fbar/F\text{,}\) and let \(R=\{\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n\}\subseteq \Fbar\) be the roots of \(f\text{.}\)
  1. Each \(\sigma\in G_f\) defines a permutation
    \begin{align*} \sigma\colon R \amp \rightarrow R\\ \alpha_i \amp\mapsto \sigma(\alpha_i)=\alpha_{i'} \text{.} \end{align*}
    There is an injective group homomorphism
    \begin{align*} \phi\colon G_f \amp \rightarrow S_n \end{align*}
    defined as follows: \(\phi(\sigma)\) is the permutation
    \begin{align*} \{1,2,\dots, n\} \amp \xrightarrow{\ \phi(\sigma)\ } \{1,2,\dots, n\}\\ i \amp \xmapsto{\hspace{20pt}} i'\text{,} \end{align*}
    where \(\sigma(\alpha_i)=\alpha_{i'}\text{.}\)
  2. The image \(\im \phi=\phi(G_f)\) is a transitive subgroup of \(S_n\) satisfying
    \begin{equation*} n\mid \abs{\phi(G_f)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    In other words, \(G_f\) is isomorphic to a transitive subgroup of \(S_n\) whose cardinality is a multiple of \(n\text{.}\)
