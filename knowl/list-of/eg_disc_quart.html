Example 1.20.6. Discriminant of quartic.

Let \(f(x)=x^4+1\in \Q[x]\text{.}\) We recognize \(f\) as the \(4\)-th cyclotomic polynomial \(\Phi_4(x)\text{.}\) Thus the roots of \(f\) are the primitive \(4\)-th roots of unity. In other words, letting \(\zeta=e^{2\pi i/8}\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} f(x)=(x-\zeta)(x-\zeta^3)(x-\zeta^5)(x-\zeta^7)\text{.} \end{equation*}
The discriminant of \(f\) is thus
\begin{align*} \Delta_f \amp = [(\zeta^3-\zeta)(\zeta^5-\zeta^3)(\zeta^7-\zeta^5)(\zeta^7-\zeta)(\zeta^7-\zeta^3)(\zeta^5-\zeta)]^2 ]\\ \amp = [(-\sqrt{2})(-\sqrt{2}i)(\sqrt{2})(-\sqrt{2}i)(2\zeta^7)(-2\zeta)]^2 \amp (\zeta^7=-\zeta^3, \zeta^5=-\zeta)\\ \amp = 2^8i^4\zeta^8\\ \amp = 256\text{.} \end{align*}
