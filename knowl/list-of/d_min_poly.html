Definition 1.5.2. Minimal polynomial.

Let \(K/F\) be a field extension, and let \(\alpha\in K\) be algebraic. The unique monic polynomial satisfying the three equivalent conditions of TheoremÂ 1.5.1 is called the minimal polynomial (or irreducible polynomial) of \(\alpha\) over \(F\text{,}\) and is denoted \(m_{\alpha, F}(x)\text{,}\) or just \(m_\alpha(x)\) if the base field \(F\) is understood.
The degree of the algebraic element \(\alpha\) over \(F\text{,}\) denoted \(\deg_F \alpha\text{,}\) is defined as the degree of the minimal polynomial of \(\alpha\text{:}\) i.e., \(\deg_F\alpha=\deg m_{\alpha, F}(x)\text{.}\)
