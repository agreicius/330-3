Theorem 1.20.6. Splitting fields of quadratics and cubics.

Let \(F\) be a field of cardinality not equal to \(2\text{,}\) let \(f(x)\in F[x]\) be a monic but not necessarily irreducible polynomial, and let \(\sqrt{\Delta_f}\in \overline{F}\) be a square-root of \(\Delta_f\text{.}\)
  1. If \(\deg f=2\text{,}\) then \(K_f=F(\sqrt{\Delta_f})\)
  2. If \(\deg f=3\text{,}\) then \(K_f=F(\alpha, \sqrt{\Delta_f})\text{,}\) where \(\alpha\in \overline{F}\) is any root of \(f\text{.}\)

Proof.

  1. Let \(f(x)=x^2+ax+b=(x-\alpha)(x-\beta)\text{.}\) We saw in ExampleÂ 1.20.5 that \(\Delta_f=b^2-4ac\text{.}\) Since \(\character F\ne 2\text{,}\) the quadratic formula applies, and we have
    \begin{align*} \{\alpha,\beta\} \amp =\left\{\frac{-b+\sqrt{b^2-4c}}{2}, \frac{-b-\sqrt{b^2-4c}}{2}\right\} \\ \amp =\left\{\frac{-b+\sqrt{\Delta_f}}{2},\frac{-b-\sqrt{\Delta_f}}{2}\right\}\text{.} \end{align*}
    It follows that
    \begin{equation*} K_f=F(\alpha,\beta)=F(\sqrt{\Delta_f})\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Let \(\alpha,\beta,\gamma\) be the roots of \(f\text{,}\) and write \(f(x)=(x-\alpha)g(x)\text{,}\) where \(g(x)=(x-\beta)(x-\gamma)\) and \(g(x)\in F(\alpha)[x]\text{.}\) By (1), we have
    \begin{align*} K_f \amp =F(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)\\ \amp =F(\alpha)(\beta,\gamma)\\ \amp =F(\alpha)(\sqrt{\Delta_g})\text{,} \end{align*}
    where \(\sqrt{\Delta_g}\in \overline{F}\) is a square-root of \(\Delta_g=(\gamma-\beta)^2\text{.}\) Since
    \begin{align*} \Delta_f\amp= (\alpha-\beta)^2(\alpha-\gamma)^2(\gamma-\beta)^2 \\ \amp = g(\alpha)^2\Delta_g\text{,} \end{align*}
    we see that \(\sqrt{\Delta_f}/g(\alpha)=\sqrt{\Delta_g}\text{,}\) and since \(g(\alpha)\in F(\alpha)\text{,}\) we conclude that
    \begin{equation*} K_f=F(\alpha,\sqrt{\Delta_g})=F(\alpha,\sqrt{\Delta_f}/g(\alpha))=F(\alpha,\sqrt{\Delta_f})\text{.} \end{equation*}
