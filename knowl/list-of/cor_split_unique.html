Corollary 1.11.2. Uniqueness of splitting fields.

Let \(F\) be a field, and let \(\mathcal{P}\subseteq F[x]\) be a collection of polynomials over \(F\text{.}\) Given any splitting fields \(K/F\) and \(K'/F\) of \(\mathcal{P}\text{,}\) there is an \(F\)-isomorphism \(\sigma\colon K\rightarrow K'\text{.}\) In other words, any two splitting fields of \(\mathcal{P}\) are isomorphic over \(F\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(K/F\) and \(K'/F\) be two splitting fields of \(\mathcal{P}\subseteq F[x]\text{,}\) and let \(A_K\) and \(A_{K'}\) be defined as
\begin{align} A_K \amp = \{\alpha\in K\colon f(\alpha)=0 \text{ for some } f\in \mathcal{P} \}\tag{1.23}\\ A_{K'} \amp = \{\alpha\in K'\colon f(\alpha)=0 \text{ for some } f\in \mathcal{P} \}\text{.}\tag{1.24} \end{align}
In other words, \(A_K\) and \(A_{K'}\) are the sets of all roots of all elements of \(\mathcal{P}\) lying in \(K\text{,}\) respectively \(K'\text{.}\) Recall that, being splitting fields, \(K\) and \(K'\) are both algebraic over \(F\) and are generated over \(F\) by the roots of elements of \(\mathcal{P}\text{:}\) i.e., we have
\begin{align*} K\amp =F(A_K) \amp K'=F(A_{K'}) \text{.} \end{align*}
Let \(L/K'\) be an algebraic closure of \(K'\text{.}\) Applying Theorem 1.11.1 to the inclusion map \(i\colon F\hookrightarrow K'\leq L\text{,}\) we get an \(F\)-embedding \(\tau\colon K\rightarrow L\text{.}\)
We claim that in fact \(\tau(K)=K'\leq L\text{,}\) in which case restricting the codomain yields an isomorphism \(\tau\colon K\rightarrow K'\text{.}\) We use the generator descriptions (1.23)–(1.24) to this end. Consider \(\tau(A_K)\) the image under \(\tau\) of the set of all roots of elements of \(\mathcal{P}\text{.}\) Since \(\tau\) is an \(F\)-embedding, it is easy to see that \(\tau(A_K)\subseteq A_{K'}\text{.}\) Indeed given \(\alpha\in A_K\text{,}\) if \(\alpha\) is a root of \(f(x)=\anpoly\in \mathcal{P}\text{,}\) then we have
\begin{align*} 0=\tau(0)\amp=\tau(f(\alpha)) \\ \amp = \tau(a_n\alpha^{n}+\cdots+a_1\alpha+a_0)\\ \amp = a_n(\tau(\alpha))^n+\cdots +a_1\tau(\alpha)+a_0 \amp (\tau\in \Hom_F(K,L))\text{.} \end{align*}
This argument shows, in general, that for any \(f\in \mathcal{P}\text{,}\) \(\tau\) maps the roots \(R_{f,K}\) of \(f\) in \(K\) to the roots \(R_{f,K'}\) of \(f\) in \(K'\text{:}\) i.e., restricting \(\tau\) to \(R_{f,K}\) yields an injective function \(\tau\colon R_{f,K}\rightarrow R_{f,K'}\text{.}\) In fact, this map is a bijection: since both \(K\) and \(K'\) split \(f\) completely, we have \(\abs{R_{f,K}}=\abs{R_{f,K'}}< \infty\text{,}\) and thus since \(\tau\) is injective it is also surjective! We conclude that \(\tau(R_{f,K})=R_{f,K'}\) for all polynomials \(f\in \mathcal{P}\text{,}\) from whence it follows immediately that \(\tau(A_K)=A_{K'}\text{.}\)
Lastly, using the fact that \(K=F(A_K)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} \tau(K)\amp =\tau(F(A_K)) \\ \amp = F(\tau(A_{K'}))\\ \amp = F(A_{K'})\\ \amp = K' \text{,} \end{align*}
as claimed. The equality \(\tau(F(A_K))=F(\tau(A_K))\) above follows pretty much directly from the minimality property of generated fields: e.g., \(\tau(F(A_K))\) is a field containing \(F\) and \(\tau(A_K)\text{,}\) thus \(F(\tau(A_K))\leq \tau(F_K(A_K)) \)
