Notation 1.4.5. Quotients of polynomial rings.

Let \(F\) be a field, let \(f(x)\) be a nonconstant polynomial in \(F[x]\text{,}\) and let \(\pi\colon F[x]\rightarrow F[x]/(f)\) be the corresponding quotient ring homomorphism.
  1. Since \(\deg f\geq 1\text{,}\) we have \((f)\cap F=(0)\text{,}\) and hence the restriction of \(\pi\) to \(F\leq F[x]\) is injective. As such \(\pi(F)\cong F\text{,}\) and we identify \(F\) with its isomorphic image \(\pi(F)\leq F[x]/(f)\text{.}\)
  2. Given \(g(x)=\bmpoly\in F[x]\) we denote \(\pi(g)=\overline{g}\) for short. We have
    \begin{align*} \overline{g} \amp =\overline{b_m}\overline{x}^m+\overline{b_{m-1}}\overline{x}^{m-1}+\cdots +\overline{b_1}\overline{x}+\overline{b_0} \amp (\pi \text{ is ring homo.})\\ \amp =b_m\overline{x}^m+b_{m-1}\overline{x}^{m-1}+\cdots +b_1\overline{x}+b_0 \amp (\text{identify } F \text{ with } \pi(F))\text{.} \end{align*}
