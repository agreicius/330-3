Theorem 1.9.1. Finite fields.

Let \(\Fpbar/\F_p\) be an algebraic closure and let \(n\) be a positive integer.
  1. There is a unique subextension \(K/\F_p\) of \(\Fpbar/\F_p\) of degree \(n\text{.}\)
  2. We have \(\abs{K}=p^n\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(f(x)=x^{p^n}-x\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{equation} K=\{\alpha\in \Fpbar\colon f(\alpha)=0\}=\{\alpha\in \Fpbar\colon \alpha^{p^n}=\alpha\}\text{.}\tag{1.16} \end{equation}
    It follows that \(K\) is a splitting field of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
