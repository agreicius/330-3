Example 1.3.9. Field of cardinality four.

Let \(f(x)=x^2+x+1\in \F_2[x]\text{.}\) Prove that \(\F_2[x]/(f)\) is a field of cardinality four.
Solution.
Denote \(\F_2=\{0,1\}\text{.}\) Since \(\deg f=2\text{,}\) it is irreducible over \(\F_2\) if and only if it has a root in \(\F_2\text{.}\) Since \(f(0)=f(1)=1\ne 0 \text{,}\) we see that \(f\) is irreducible. Using CorollaryÂ 1.3.8, we conclude that \(K=\F_2[x]/(f)\) is field, and that \(B=\{\overline{x}, 1\}\) is a basis of \(K\) over \(\F_2\text{.}\) It follows that every element of \(K\) can be uniquely expressed as \(a\overline{x}+b\text{,}\) with \(a,b\in \F_2\text{.}\) There are exactly four such expressions:
\begin{equation*} 0=0\overline{x}+0, 1=0\overline{x}+1, \overline{x}=1\overline{x}+0, \overline{x}+1=1\overline{x}+1 \text{.} \end{equation*}
