Example 1.12.4. Separable polynomial.

Let \(\overline{\Q}\) be the algebraic closure of \(\Q\) in \(\C\text{.}\) The polynomials \(x^2-1\) and \(x^2+x+1\) are separable polynomials of \(\Q[x]\) since they factor into as
\begin{align*} x^2-1 \amp= (x-1)(x+1)\amp x^2+x+1=(x-e^{2\pi i/3})(x-e^{4\pi/3}) \end{align*}
over \(\overline{\Q}\text{.}\) (Note: \(e^{2\pi i/3}\ne e^{4\pi i/3}\text{.}\))
The polynomial \(x^2-4x+4\) is not separable as it has a repeated root:
\begin{equation*} x^2-4x+4=(x-2)^2\text{.} \end{equation*}
