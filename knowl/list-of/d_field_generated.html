Definition 1.2.6. Generated field.

Given a field extension \(F\leq K\) and a subset \(A\subseteq K\) the field generated over \(F\) by \(A\text{,}\) denoted \(F(A)\text{,}\) is defined as
\begin{equation*} F(A)=\bigcap_{\substack{F\leq K'\leq K\\ A\subseteq K'}}K'\text{.} \end{equation*}
Equivalently, \(F(A)\) is the “smallest” subfield of \(K\) containing the set \(F\) and \(A\text{:}\) that is, if \(F\leq K'\leq K\) and \(A\subseteq K'\text{,}\) then \(F(A)\leq K'\text{.}\)
When \(A=\{\alpha_1, \alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n\}\) (resp., \(A=\{\alpha_1, \alpha_2,\dots, \}\)), we denote \(F(A)\) as \(F(\alpha_1, \alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n)\) (resp. \(F(\alpha_1, \alpha_2,\dots)\)).
