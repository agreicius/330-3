Example 1.3.8. Degree of rational functions.

Let \(F\) be a field and consider the field extension \(F\leq F(x)\text{,}\) where \(F(x)\) is the field of rational functions in one variable over \(F\text{.}\) Compute \([F(x)\colon F]\text{.}\)
Solution.
We claim \([F(x)\colon F]=\infty\text{.}\) To prove the claim it is enough, thanks to TheoremÂ 1.3.3, to show that there is an infinite linearly independent set. To this end, we claim that the set \(S=\{1,x,x^2\dots \}\subseteq F(x)\) of all nonnegative powers of \(x\) is linearly independent over \(F\text{.}\) Indeed, a finite linear combination of elements of $x$ is nothing more than a polynomial \(f(x)=\anpoly\text{,}\) where \(a_i\in F\) for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\) By definition of the polynomial ring \(F[x]\text{,}\) which we think of as a subset of \(F(x)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} \anpoly=0\iff a_i=0 \text{ for all } 1\leq i\leq n\text{.} \end{equation*}
This proves \(S\) is linearly independent, and hence that \(F(x)/F\) is an infinite extension.
