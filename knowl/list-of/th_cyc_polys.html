Theorem 1.19.5. Cyclotomic polynomials.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer.
  1. \(\Phi_n(x)\in \Z[x]\text{.}\)
  2. \(\Phi_n(x)\) is irreducible over \(\Q\text{.}\)

Proof.

  1. We first prove that for all positive integers \(n\) we have
    \begin{align*} x^n-1 \amp =\prod_{d\mid n}\Phi_d(x)\text{.} \end{align*}
    This result is made official in TheoremÂ 1.19.8. The proof is by strong induction. The base case \(n=1\) is true as \(\zeta=1\) is the only primitive first root of unity, and thus \(\Phi_1(x)=(x-1)=x^1-1\text{.}\)
