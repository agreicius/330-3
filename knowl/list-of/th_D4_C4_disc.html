Theorem 1.21.11. \(C_4\) or \(D_4\text{:}\) partial check.

Let \(f(x)\in \Q[x]\) be a monic separable irreducible polynomial of degree \(4\text{.}\) Using the notation of TheoremÂ 1.21.6, if \(G_f\cong C_4\text{,}\) then \(\Delta_f> 0\text{.}\)

Proof.

We take \(\overline{\Q}\) to be the algebraic closure of \(\Q\) in \(\C\text{.}\) Assume that \(G_f\cong C_4\text{.}\) Let \(\alpha\) be a root of \(f(x)\text{.}\) Since \([K_f\colon \Q]=[\Q(\alpha)\colon \Q]=4\text{,}\) we have \(K_f=\Q(\alpha)\text{,}\) and thus all roots of \(f\) lie in \(\Q(\alpha)\text{.}\)
If \(\alpha\) is real, then since every other root of \(f\) is an element of \(\Q(\alpha)\text{,}\) all roots of \(f\) are real. It follows easily in this case that \(\Delta_f> 0\text{.}\)
The only other possibility is that all the roots are imaginary, in which case the distinct roots can be described as \(\alpha,\overline{\alpha},\beta,\overline{\beta}\text{,}\) and we have
\begin{align*} \Delta_f \amp = (\alpha-\overline{\alpha})^2(\beta-\overline{\beta})^2 (\alpha-\beta)^2(\overline{\alpha}-\overline{\beta})^2(\alpha-\overline{\beta})^2(\overline{\alpha}-\beta)^2\\ \amp= (2i\operatorname{Im} \alpha)^2(2i\operatorname{Im} \beta)^2[(\alpha-\beta)(\overline{\alpha-\beta})]^2[(\alpha-\overline{\beta})(\overline{\alpha-\overline{\beta}})]^2\\ \amp= (2i\operatorname{Im} \alpha)^2(2i\operatorname{Im} \beta)^2\abs{\alpha-\beta}^4\abs{\alpha-\overline{\beta}}^4\\ \amp = 4(\operatorname{Im} \alpha)^2(\operatorname{Im} \beta)^2\abs{\alpha-\beta}^4\abs{\alpha-\overline{\beta}}^4\\ \amp > 0\text{.} \end{align*}
