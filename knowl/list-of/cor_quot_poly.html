Corollary 1.4.8. Quotients of polynomial rings.

Let \(F\) be a field, let \(f(x)\) be an irreducible polynomial of degree \(n\text{,}\) and let \(K=F[x]/(f)\text{.}\) Following Notation 1.4.5, we identify \(F\) with its image in \(K\) under the quotient map.
  1. \(K\) is a field and the extension \(K/F\) is simple. In fact, we have \(K=F[\overline{x}]=F(\overline{x})\text{.}\)
  2. We have \([K\colon F]=\deg f=n\text{.}\)

Proof.

The corollary is a direct consequence of Theorem 1.4.7.
