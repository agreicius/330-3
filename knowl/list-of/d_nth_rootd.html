Definition 1.19.1. Roots of unity.

Let \(F\) be a field. The \(n\)-th roots of unity of \(F\), denoted \(\mu_n(F)\text{,}\) is defined as
\begin{equation*} \mu_n(F)=\{\alpha\colon F\colon \alpha^n=1\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Given an algebraic closure of \(\overline{F}/F\text{,}\) we will often write simply \(\mu_n\) for \(\mu_{n}(\overline{F})\text{.}\) Note that \(\mu_n(K)\leq \mu_n\) for every intermediate field \(F\leq K\leq \overline{F}\text{.}\)
A primitive \(n\)-th root of unity is an element \(\zeta\in \mu_n(\overline{F})\) whose multiplicative order \(\ord \zeta\) is equal to \(n\text{:}\) i.e., \(n\) is the smallest positive integer such that \(\zeta^n=1\text{.}\)
A cyclotomic extension is a field extension of the form \(F(\zeta)/F\text{,}\) where \(\zeta\in \overline{F}\) is an \(n\)-th root of unity for some positive integer \(n\text{.}\)
