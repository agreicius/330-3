Theorem 1.21.8. Cubic resolvent.

Let \(f(x)=x^4+ax^3+bx+c\in F[x]\) be a degree-4 polynomial, and let \(R_f(x)\) be its cubic resolvent.
  1. \(R_f(x)\in F[x]\text{.}\)
  2. \(R_f(x)=x^3-bx^2+(ac-4d)x-(a^2d+c^2-4bd)\text{.}\) In particular, we have the special case
    \begin{equation} f(x)=x^4+cx+d \implies R_f(x)=x^3-4dx-c^2\text{.}\tag{1.38} \end{equation}
  3. The discriminant of \(R_f(x)\) is equal to the discriminant of \(f(x)\text{.}\) In particular, \(R_f(x)\) has no repeated roots if and only if \(f(x)\) has no repeated roots.
