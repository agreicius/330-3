Theorem 1.6.9. Finite extension theorem.

Let \(K/F\) be a field extension. The following statements are equivalent.
  1. \(K/F\) is finite.
  2. \(K/F\) is finitely generated and algebraic.
  3. \(K=F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_m)\text{,}\) where \(\alpha_i\in K\) is algebraic over \(F\text{.}\) In other words, \(K\) is generated over \(F\) by finitely many algebraic elements.

Proof.

We prove the “cycle of implications” (1)\(\implies\)(2)\(\implies\)(3)\(\implies\) (1)

Implication: (1)\(\implies\)(2).

Assume \(K/F\) is finite, of dimension \(n\text{.}\)
  • By Theorem 1.6.5, since \(K/F\) is finite, it is algebraic.
  • We claim \(K/F\) is finitely generated. Let \(B=\{\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n\}\) be a basis of \(K\) as an \(F\)-vector space. Since \(\Span_F B=K\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} K=\{c_1\alpha_1+c_2\alpha_2+\cdots +c_n\alpha_n\colon c_i\in F\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    It follows that \(K=F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n)\text{,}\) and hence is finitely generated (by the elements \(\alpha_i\)). Indeed, since \(F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n)\) is a subfield of \(K\) containing \(F\) and \(\alpha_i\) for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{,}\) it must contain all \(F\)-linear combinations of the \(\alpha_i\text{.}\) But then we have
    \begin{equation*} K=\{c_1\alpha_1+c_2\alpha_2+\cdots +c_n\alpha_n\colon c_i\in F\}\leq F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n)\leq K\text{,} \end{equation*}
    and conclude that \(K=F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n)\text{.}\)

Implication: (2)\(\implies\)(3).

Assume \(K/F\) is finitely generated and algebraic. Since \(K/F\) is finitely generated, there are elements \(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_m\) such that \(K=F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_m)\text{.}\) Since \(K/F\) is algebraic, each element \(\alpha_i\) is algebraic over \(F\text{.}\) Thus \(K=F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_m)\text{,}\) and each \(\alpha_i\) is algebraic over \(F\text{,}\) as desired.

Implication: (3)\(\implies\)(1).

We will show that any field extension generated by finitely many algebraic elements is finite. The proof will proceed by induction on the number \(m\) of generators of the field.
Base step: \(m=1\text{.}\) Assume \(K=F(\alpha)\text{,}\) where \(\alpha\) is algebraic over \(F\text{.}\) By Theorem 1.4.14, we have \(\dim_F K< \infty\text{.}\) Thus \(K/F\) is finite.
Induction step: assume any field extension generated over the base field by \(m\) algebraic elements is a finite extension. Now suppose we have \(K=F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_{m+1})\text{,}\) where each \(\alpha_i\) is algebraic over \(F\text{.}\) If \(\alpha_i\in F\) for all \(i\text{,}\) then \(F=K\text{,}\) in which case \(K\) is finite over \(F\text{.}\) Otherwise, we may assume without loss of generality that \(\alpha_1\in K-F\text{.}\) Since \(\alpha_1\) is algebraic over \(F\text{,}\) the extension \(F(\alpha_1)/F\) is finite by the base step. Let \(K'=F(\alpha_1)\text{.}\) We have a tower of fields \(K/K'/F\text{,}\) and thus by the tower law, the extension \(K/F\) is finite if and only if \(K/K'\) is finite and \(K'/F\) is finite. We know already that \(K'=F(\alpha_1)\) is finite over \(F\text{.}\) As for the extension \(K/K'\) it is easy to see that
\begin{equation*} K=F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_{m+1})=F(\alpha_1)(\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_{m+1})=K'(\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_{m+1})\text{.} \end{equation*}
Furthermore, since each \(\alpha_i\) is satisfies a nonzero polynomial \(f_i(x)\in F[x]\subseteq K'[x]\text{,}\) we see that each \(\alpha_i\) is algebraic over \(K'\text{.}\) Thus the extension \(K/K'\) is generated over \(K'\) by the \(m\) algebraic elements \(\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_{m+1}\text{.}\) By induction \(K/K'\) is finite. The tower law now implies that \(K/F\) is finite.
