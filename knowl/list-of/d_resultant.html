Definition 1.21.7. Cubic resolvent.

Let \(f(x)\in F[x]\) be a degree-4 polynomial that factors over an algebraic closure \(\overline{F}\) as
\begin{equation*} f(x)=(x-r_1)(x-r_2)(x-r_3)(x-r_4)\text{.} \end{equation*}
Define
\begin{align*} \theta_1 \amp =r_1r_2+r_3r_4 \\ \theta_2 \amp =r_1r_3+r_2r_4\\ \theta_3 \amp =r_1r_4+r_2r_3\text{.} \end{align*}
The cubic resolvent of \(f\text{,}\) denoted \(R_f(x)\text{,}\) is the polynomial
\begin{equation*} R_f(x)=(x-\theta_1)(x-\theta_2)(x-\theta_3)\text{.} \end{equation*}
