Theorem 1.9.11. Subextension structure of \(\Fpbar\).

Let \(\Fpbar/\F_p\) be an algebraic closure. For each positive integer \(n\text{,}\) we denote by \(\F_{p^{n}}\) the unique subfield of \(\Fpbar\) of cardinality \(n\text{.}\) Given positive integers \(m\) and \(n\text{,}\) we have \(\F_{p^m}\leq \F_{p^n}\) if and only if \(m\) divides \(n\text{.}\) Using logical shorthand:
\begin{equation*} \F_{p^m}\leq \F_{p^n}\iff m\mid n\text{.} \end{equation*}

Proof.

We prove each implication separately.

Implication:\(\implies\).

Assume \(\F_{p^m}\leq \F_{p^n}\text{.}\) Applying the tower law to the tower \(\F_p\leq \F_{p^m}\leq \F_{p^n}\text{,}\) we see that \(m=[\F_{p^m}\colon \F_p]\) divides \(n=[\F_{p^n}\colon \F_p]\text{.}\)

Implication: \(\impliedby\).

If \(m\mid n\text{,}\) then \(n=mr\) for some integer \(r\geq 1\text{.}\) We wish to show the implication
\begin{equation*} \alpha\in \F_{p^m}\implies \alpha\in \F_{p^n}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Recall our explicit descriptions of the two fields as subfields of \(\Fpbar\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \F_{p^m}\amp = \{\alpha\in \Fpbar\colon \alpha^{p^m}=\alpha\} \\ \F_{p^n}\amp = \{\alpha\in \Fpbar\colon \alpha^{p^n}=\alpha\} \text{.} \end{align*}
Define \(\phi\colon \Fpbar\rightarrow \Fpbar\) as \(\phi(\alpha)=\alpha^{p^{m}}\text{,}\) and let
\begin{equation*} \phi^r=\underset{r \text{ times}}{\underbrace{\phi\circ\phi\circ\cdots \circ\phi}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
An easy induction argument shows that \(\phi^r(\alpha)=\alpha^{p^{mr}}\text{.}\) Thus we have
\begin{align*} \alpha\in \F_{p^m}\amp \implies \alpha^{p^{m}}=\alpha\\ \amp\implies \phi(\alpha)=\alpha\\ \amp \implies \phi^r(\alpha)=\alpha\\ \amp \implies \alpha^{p^{mr}}=\alpha\\ \amp \implies \alpha^{p^{n}}=\alpha\\ \amp \implies \alpha\in \F_{p^{n}}\text{,} \end{align*}
as desired.
