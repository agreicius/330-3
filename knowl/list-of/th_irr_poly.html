Theorem 1.4.13. Minimal polynomial.

Let \(K/F\) be a field extension, let \(\alpha\in K\) be algebraic, and let \(\phi\colon F[x]\rightarrow K\) be the evaluation at \(\alpha\) map. There is a unique polynomial \(f(x)=x^n+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots +a_1x+a_0\) satisfying the following equivalent conditions.
  1. \(f(x)\) is monic and satisfies \(\ker \phi=(f)\text{.}\)
  2. \(f(x)\) is a monic polynomial of minimal degree satisfying \(f(\alpha)=0\text{.}\)
  3. \(f(x)\) is a monic irreducible polynomial satisfying \(f(\alpha)=0\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(\phi\colon F[x]\rightarrow K\) be the evaluation at \(\alpha\) map. Since \(\alpha\) is algebraic, according to TheoremÂ 1.4.12 \(\ker\phi\) is nonzero and generated by an irreducible polynomial. Although the generator of \(\ker\phi\) is not in general unique, there is a unique monic irreducible generator \(f(x)\text{.}\) This gives us the unique existence of a polynomial as described in (1).
Continue to assume that \(f\) is the unique monic generator of \(\ker \phi\text{.}\) The equivalence of conditions (1)-(3) follows from the observation that any generator of \(\ker\phi\) must be irreducible (since \(\ker\phi\) is a nonzero prime ideal) and that, given a polynomial \(g\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} \ker\phi=(g) \amp \iff g \text{ is an element of } \ker\phi \text{ of minimal degree}\\ \amp \iff g \text{ is a polynomial of minimal degree satisfying } g(\alpha)=0\\ \amp \iff g \text{ is an irreducible polynomial satisfying } g(\alpha)=0\text{.} \end{align*}
