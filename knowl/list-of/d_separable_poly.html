Definition 1.12.3. Separable polynomial.

Let \(F\) be a field. A nonzero polynomial \(f(x)\in F[x]\) is separable if all its roots (in some algebraic closure of \(F\)) are simple: equivalently, if \(f\) has has \(n=\deg f\) distinct roots. A polynomial is inseparable if it is not separable.
