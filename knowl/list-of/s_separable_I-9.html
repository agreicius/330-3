Example 1.12.8. Separability and derivative.

Let \(p\) be a prime integer. Use TheoremÂ 1.12.7 to prove that the polynomial \(f(x)=x^p-x\in \F_p[x]\) is separable.
Solution.
We have \(f'(x)=px^{p-1}-1=-1\text{,}\) since \(p=0\in \F_p\text{.}\) Since \(f'=-1\) is a unit in \(\F_p[x]\text{,}\) it follows that \((f,f')=1\text{.}\)
