Example 1.3.3. Simple extensions.

  1. The extension \(\C/\R\) is simple, since \(\C=\R(i)\text{.}\)
  2. For any field \(F\text{,}\) the field of rational functions \(F(x)\) in one variable is a simple field extension of \(F\text{.}\)
    On the other hand it is possible to show that the field of rational functions \(F(x,y)\) in two variables is not simple: i.e., we do not have \(F(x,y)=F(q)\) for any rational function \(q(x,y)=f(x,y)/g(x,y)\text{.}\)
