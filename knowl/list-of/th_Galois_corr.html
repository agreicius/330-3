Theorem 1.16.5. Galois correspondence.

Let \(L/F\) be a finite Galois extension.
  1. The two maps of the Galois correspondence for \(L/F\) are inverses of one another, defining a bijection between the set of intermediate fields of \(L/F\) and the set of subgroups of \(\Gal(L/F)\text{.}\)
  2. Given an intermediate field \(K\text{,}\) the extension \(L/K\) is Galois and \(\Aut_K(L)=\Gal(L/K)\text{.}\)
  3. Given a subgroup \(H\leq \Gal(L/F)\text{,}\) we have \([L\colon L^H]=\abs{H}\) and \([L^H\colon F]=[\Gal(L/F)\colon H]\text{.}\)
  4. The Galois correspondence is inclusion reversing. In more detail, we have
    \begin{equation*} K\leq K' \iff \Aut_{K'}(L)\leq \Aut_K(L) \end{equation*}
    for all intermediate fields \(K\) and \(K'\text{,}\) and
    \begin{equation*} H\leq H' \iff L^{H'}\leq L^H\text{,} \end{equation*}
    for all subgroups \(H\) and \(H'\text{.}\)
