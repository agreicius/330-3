Corollary 1.9.8. Existence of irreducibles.

For all positive integers \(n\text{,}\) there is an irreducible polynomial of \(\F_p[x]\) of degree \(n\text{.}\)

Proof.

Fix a positive integer \(n\text{.}\) By Theorem 1.9.1 there is an extension \(K/\F_p\) of degree \(n\text{.}\) By [cross-reference to target(s) "th_finite_cylcic" missing or not unique], we have \(K=\F_p(\alpha)\) for some \(\alpha\text{.}\) By Theorem 1.4.14 we have \(K\cong \F_p[x]/(f(x))\) for some irreducible polynomial of degree \(n\text{.}\) In particular, there is at least one irreducible polynomial of degree \(n\text{.}\)
