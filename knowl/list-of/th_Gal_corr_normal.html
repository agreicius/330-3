Theorem 1.18.4. Galois correspondence: normality.

Let \(L/F\) be a finite Galois extension, and let \(G=\Gal(L/F)\text{.}\) Suppose the intermediate field \(K\leq L\) corresponds to the subgroup \(H\leq G\) under the Galois correspondence: i.e., we have
\begin{align*} K \amp = L^H \amp H\amp =\Aut_K(L)\text{.} \end{align*}
  1. Given \(\sigma\in G\text{,}\) the Galois conjugate \(K'=\sigma(K)\) corresponds to \(H'=\sigma H \sigma^{-1}\text{,}\) the conjugate of \(H\) by \(\sigma\text{:}\) i.e., we have
    \begin{align*} \sigma(K)\amp =L^{\sigma H\sigma^{-1}} \amp \sigma H \sigma^{-1}=\Aut_{\sigma(K)}(L) \end{align*}
  2. The extension \(K/F\) is normal if and only if \(H\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) Using logical shorthand:
    \begin{equation} K/F \text{ normal} \iff H\normalin G \text{.}\tag{1.33} \end{equation}
  3. Assume \(K/F\) is normal, and write \(N=\Aut_{K}(L)\text{.}\) The map
    \begin{align*} \sigma\colon G\amp \rightarrow \Gal(K/F)\\ \sigma \amp \mapsto \sigma\vert_K \end{align*}
    gives rise to an isomorphism
    \begin{align*} G/N \amp \isomto \Gal(K/F)\\ \sigma N \amp \mapsto \sigma\vert_{K}\text{.} \end{align*}
    In particular, the Galois group of \(K/F\) is isomorphic to \(G/N\text{:}\) i.e.,
    \begin{equation} \Gal(K/F)\cong G/N\text{.}\tag{1.34} \end{equation}
