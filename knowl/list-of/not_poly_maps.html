Notation 1.3.6. Maps between polynomial rings.

A ring homomorphism \(\phi\colon R\rightarrow S\) extends naturally to a ring homomorphism \(R[x] \longrightarrow S[x]\) that sends an \(R\)-polynomial \(\anpoly\) to the \(S\)-polynomial \(\phi(a_n)x^n+\phi(a_{n-1})x^{n-1}+\cdots +\phi(a_1)x+\phi(a_0)\text{.}\) By abuse of notation, we will also denote this map of polynonmial rings as \(\phi\text{.}\) Thus, given \(f(x)=\anpoly\) we will write
\begin{equation*} \phi(f)=\phi(a_n)x^n+\phi(a_{n-1})x^{n-1}+\cdots +\phi(a_1)x+\phi(a_0)\text{.} \end{equation*}
