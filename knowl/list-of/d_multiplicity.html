Definition 1.12.1. Multiplicity of roots.

Let \(K/F\) be an algebraic closure of \(F\text{.}\) Given a nonzero polynomial \(f(x)\in F[x]\text{,}\) we can factor \(f(x)\) in the form
\begin{equation} f(x)=c(x-\alpha_1)^{n_1}(x-\alpha_2)^{n_2}\cdots (x-\alpha_r)^{n_r}, c\in F, \alpha_i\in K\tag{1.23} \end{equation}
where \(\alpha_i\ne \alpha_j\) for all \(i\ne j\text{:}\) i.e., the \(\alpha_i\) are the distinct roots of \(f\) in \(K\text{.}\) For each \(1\leq i\leq r\text{,}\) we call the power \(n_i\) the multiplicity of the root \(\alpha_i\text{.}\) The root \(\alpha_i\) is called a simple root if \(n_i=1\text{;}\) it is called a repeated root if \(n_i\geq 2\text{.}\)
