Definition 1.20.1. Galois group of polynomial.

Let \(f(x)\in F[x]\) be a separable polynomial, and let \(\Fbar/F\) be an algebraic closure. Denote by \(K_f\) the splitting field of \(f\text{:}\) i.e., we have
\begin{equation*} K=F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n)\leq \overline{F}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n\) are the roots of \(f\) over \(\overline{F}\text{.}\) We call \(\Gal(K_f/F)\) the Galois group of \(f\), and write \(G_f=\Gal(K_f/F)\text{.}\)
