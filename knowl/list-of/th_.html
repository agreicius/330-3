Theorem 1.19.12. Galois theory of finite fields.

Let \(p\) be a prime integer, let \(\Fpbar/\F_p\) be an algebraic closure of \(\F_p\text{,}\) and let \(\phi\colon \Fpbar\rightarrow \Fpbar\) be the Frobenius automorphism: i.e., \(\phi(\alpha)=\alpha^p\) for all \(\alpha\in \Fpbar\text{.}\) For each positive integer \(n\text{,}\) let \(\F_{p^n}\) be the unique subfield of \(\Fpbar\) of degree \(n\) over \(\F_p\text{.}\)
  1. For all positive integers \(n\text{,}\) the extension \(\F_{p^n}/\F_p\) is Galois, and \(\Gal(\F_{p^{n}}/\F_{p})\) is cyclic, generated by the restriction of \(\phi\) to \(\F_{p^n}\text{.}\) In other words, we have
    \begin{equation*} \Gal(\F_{p^n}/\F_p)=\angvec{\phi}\cong \Z/n\Z\text{,} \end{equation*}
    where by abuse of notation we write \(\phi\) for the restriction \(\phi\vert_{\F_{p^n}}\text{.}\)
  2. For any positive integers \(n, d\) with \(d\mid n\text{,}\) the extension \(\F_{p^n}/\F_{p^d}\) is Galois, and \(\Gal(\F_{p^n}/\F_{p^{d}})\) is cyclic generated by the restriction of \(\phi^d\) to \(\F_{p^n}\text{.}\) In other words, we have we have
    \begin{equation*} \Gal(\F_{p^n}/\F_{p^d})=\angvec{\phi^d}\cong \Z/(n/d)\Z\text{,} \end{equation*}
    where by abuse of notation we write \(\phi\) for the restriction \(\phi\vert_{\F_{p^n}}\text{.}\)

Proof.
