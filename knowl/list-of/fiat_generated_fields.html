Fiat 1.3.2. Generated fields convention.

Let \(F\) be a field. In order to speak of a field of the form \(K=F(\alpha)\) or \(K=F(\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n)\text{,}\) we technically need to introduce a larger “ambient” field \(L/F\) where these elements \(\alpha\) and \(\alpha_i\) are drawn from.
In situations where this ambient field \(L\) does not play an important role, we reserve the right to omit it from our discussion. For example, if introducing a simple field extension \(F(\alpha)/F\) without further details, it is assumed that in fact \(F\leq F(\alpha)\leq L\) for some larger field \(L\text{.}\)
