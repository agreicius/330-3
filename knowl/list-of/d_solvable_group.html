Definition 1.22.7. Solvable group.

A finite group \(G\) is solvable if there exists a sequence of subgroups
\begin{equation*} \{1\}=G_0\normalin G_1\normalin \dots \normalin G_n=G \end{equation*}
where for all \(1\leq k\leq n\) the subgroup \(G_{k-1}\) is normal in \(G_k\) and the quotient group \(G_k/G_{k-1}\) is abelian.
