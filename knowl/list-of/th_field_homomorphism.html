Theorem 1.1.4. Ring homomorphism from field.

Let \(F\) be a field and let \(R\) be a nonzero ring. If \(\phi\colon F\rightarrow R\) is a ring homomorphism, then \(\phi\) is injective.

Proof.

Since \(\phi\) is a ring homomorphism, its kernel \(\ker \phi\) is an ideal in \(F\text{.}\) Since \(F\) is a field, its only ideals are \((0)\) and \(F\text{.}\) Since \(\phi(1_F)=1_R\ne 0_R\) (see FiatÂ 1.1.1), we cannot have \(\ker\phi=F\text{.}\) Thus \(\ker\phi=(0)\) and \(\phi\) is injective.
