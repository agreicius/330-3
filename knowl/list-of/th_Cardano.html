Let \(F\) be a field satisfying \(\character F\ne 2,3\text{,}\) and let \(\zeta\in \overline{F}\) be a primitive cube-root of unity. Given a cubic polynomial \(f\) of the form \(f(x)=x^3+ax+b\in F[x]\text{,}\) the roots \(\alpha, \beta,\gamma\in \overline{F}\) of \(f\) can be expressed as
\begin{align} \alpha \amp =\frac{1}{3}(\theta_1+\theta_2)\amp \beta \amp =\frac{1}{3}(\theta_1\, \zeta+\theta_2\zeta^2)\amp \gamma \amp =\frac{1}{3}(\theta_1\, \zeta^2+\theta_2\zeta)\text{,}\tag{1.44} \end{align}
where \(\theta_1\) is any cube-root of
\begin{equation*} -\frac{27b}{2}+\frac{3}{2}\sqrt{-3\Delta_f} \end{equation*}
and \(\theta_2\) is the unique cube-root of
\begin{equation*} -\frac{27b}{2}-\frac{3}{2}\sqrt{-3\Delta_f} \end{equation*}
that also satisfies
\begin{equation*} \theta_1\theta_2=-3a\text{.} \end{equation*}
In particular, all roots of \(f\) can be expressed in the form
\begin{equation} \frac{1}{3}\left( \sqrt[3]{ -\frac{27b}{2}+\frac{3}{2}\sqrt{-3\Delta_f}}+\sqrt[3]{ -\frac{27b}{2}-\frac{3}{2}\sqrt{-3\Delta_f}}\right)\text{.}\tag{1.45} \end{equation}
