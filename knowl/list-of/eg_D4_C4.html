Example 1.21.14. \(D_4\) or \(C_4\).

Prove that the given quartic polynomial \(f(x)\in \Q[x]\) is irreducible and determine a subgroup of \(S_4\) that is isomorphic to \(G_f\text{.}\) We have provided the discriminant and cubic resolvent.
  1. \(f(x)=x^4+3x+3\text{,}\) \(\Delta_f=21\cdot 15^2\text{,}\) \(R_f(x)=x^3-12x-9\text{.}\)
  2. \(f(x)=x^5+5x+5\text{,}\) \(\Delta_f=5\cdot 55^2\text{,}\) \(R_f(x)=x^3-20x-25\text{.}\)
