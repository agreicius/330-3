Example 1.2.8. Field generated by element.

Consider the extension \(\R/\Q\text{.}\) Show that \(\Q(\sqrt{2})=\{a+b\sqrt{2}\colon a,b\in \Q\}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Let \(L=\{a+b\sqrt{2}\colon a,b\in \Q\}\text{.}\) We follow ProcedureÂ 1.2.7 to show \(\Q(\sqrt{2})=L\text{.}\)
  1. We have seen earlier that \(L\) is a subfield of \(\R\text{,}\) and clearly it contains \(\Q\) and \(\sqrt{2}\text{.}\)
  2. Suppose \(K'\) is a subfield of \(\R\) containing \(\Q\) and \(\sqrt{2}\text{.}\) We must show that \(L\subseteq K'\text{.}\) Take any \(a+b\sqrt{2}\in L\text{.}\) Since by definition \(a,b\in \Q\text{,}\) and since by assumption \(K'\) contains \(\Q\) and \(\sqrt{2}\text{,}\) we have \(a,b,\sqrt{2}\in K'\text{.}\) Since \(K'\) is a subring of \(\R\text{,}\) and hence closed under addition and multiplication, we have \(a+b\sqrt{2}\in K'\text{,}\) as desired.
