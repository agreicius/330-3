Example 1.11.6. Automorphism.

The condition that \(K/F\) be algebraic is indeed a necessary one for TheoremÂ 1.11.5. Consider by contrast the transcendental extension \(F(x)/F\text{,}\) where as usual \(F(x)\) is the field of rational functions over \(F\text{.}\) It is easy to see that the map
\begin{align*} \sigma\colon F(x) \amp \rightarrow F(x) \\ f(x)/g(x) \amp \mapsto f(x^2)/g(x^2) \end{align*}
is an \(F\)-embedding, but is not surjective, hence not an automorphism.
