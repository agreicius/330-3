Corollary 1.11.2. Uniqueness of splitting fields.

Let \(F\) be a field, and let \(\mathcal{P}\subseteq F[x]\) be a collection of polynomials over \(F\text{.}\) Any two splitting fields of \(\mathcal{P}\) are isomorphic.

Proof.

Let \(K/F\) and \(K'/F\) be two splitting fields of \(\mathcal{P}\subseteq F[x]\text{.}\) Let \(A_K\) and \(A_{K'}\) be defined as
\begin{align*} A_K \amp = \{\alpha\in K\colon f(\alpha)=0 \text{ for some } f\in \mathcal{P} \}\\ A_{K'} \amp = \{\alpha\in K'\colon f(\alpha)=0 \text{ for some } f\in \mathcal{P} \}\text{.} \end{align*}
In other words, \(A_K\) and \(A_{K'}\) are the sets of all roots of all elements of \(\mathcal{P}\) lying in \(K\text{,}\) respectively \(K'\text{.}\) Recall that, being splitting fields \(K\) and \(K'\) are both algebraic over \(F\) and are generated over \(F\) by the roots of elements of \(\mathcal{P}\text{:}\) i.e., we have
\begin{align*} K\amp =F(A_K) \amp K'=F(A_{K'}) \text{.} \end{align*}
