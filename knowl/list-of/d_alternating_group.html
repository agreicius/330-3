Definition 1.20.8. Alternating group.

Given \(\sigma\in S_n\text{,}\) its sign (or parity), denoted \(\sgn \sigma\text{,}\) is defined as \(\sgn \sigma=(-1)^n\text{,}\) where \(\sigma\) can be written as a product of \(n\) \(2\)-cycles. The map
\begin{align*} \sgn\colon S_n \amp\rightarrow \{\pm 1\}\\ \sigma \amp \mapsto \sgn \sigma \end{align*}
is a surjective group homomorphism. We define the alternating subgroup of \(S_n\text{,}\) denoted \(A_n\text{,}\) to be the kernel of \(\sgn\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} A_n=\ker\sgn=\{\sigma\in S_n\colon \sgn \sigma=1\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
