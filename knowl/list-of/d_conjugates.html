Definition 1.17.2. Galois conjugates and fix subgroups.

Let \(L/F\) be a finite Galois extension, and let \(G=\Gal(L/F)\text{.}\) Given \(\alpha\in L\text{,}\) a Galois conjugate (or just conjugate) of \(\alpha\) is an element of the form \(\sigma(\alpha)\text{,}\) where \(\sigma\in G\text{.}\) We denote by \(O_\alpha\) the set of all conjugates of \(\alpha\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} O_\alpha=\{\sigma(\alpha)\colon \sigma\in G\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
The stabilizer subgroup of \(\alpha\text{,}\) denoted \(\Stab_G(\alpha)\text{,}\) is the set of all elements of \(G\) that fix \(\alpha\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \Stab_G(\alpha)=\{\sigma\in G\colon \sigma(\alpha)=\alpha\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
