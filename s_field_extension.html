    Section 1.2 Field extension

    Subsection Field extensions

    Definition 1.2.1. Subfield and field extension.

    A field extension is a pair of fields \((F,K)\) such that \(F\) is a subring of \(K\text{.}\) We say \(F\) is a subfield of \(K\) in this case, and that \(K\) is an extension of \(F\text{,}\) and denote this relation as \(F\leq K\text{.}\)
    As alternative notation to \(F\leq K\) we will also write \(K/F\text{,}\) and more graphically,
    to indicate that \(K\) is a field extension of \(F\text{.}\)

    Definition 1.2.2. Towers and subextensions.

    A tower of fields is a chain of one or more field extensions \(K_0\leq K_1\leq\dots \leq K_n\text{.}\) Similar notational conventions as in Definition 1.2.1 apply to towers of fields.
    Given a tower of fields \(L/K/F\text{,}\) we call \(K\) an intermediate field of the field extension \(L/F\text{,}\) and we call \(K/F\) a subextension of \(L/F\text{.}\)

    Example 1.2.3. Tower of fields.

    The rational, real, and complex comprise a familiar tower of fields
    \begin{equation*} \Q\leq \R\leq \C\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Definition 1.2.4. Prime subfield.

    Let \(K\) be a field. The prime subfield of \(K\) is the subfield \(F\leq K\) defined as the intersection of all subfields of \(K\text{:}\) i.e.,
    \begin{equation*} F=\bigcap_{\substack{K'\leq K \\ K' \text{ a field}}} K'\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Theorem 1.2.5. Prime subfields and characteristic.

    Let \(K\) be a field, and let \(F\) be its prime subfield.
    1. We have \(\character K=0\) or \(\character K=p\text{,}\) where \(p\) is a prime integer.
    2. If \(\character K=0\text{,}\) then \(F\cong \Q\text{.}\)
    3. If \(\character K=p> 0\text{,}\) then \(F\cong \F_p\text{.}\)

    Proof.

    Let \(\phi\colon \Z\rightarrow K\) be defined as \(\phi(m)=m\cdot 1_K\text{.}\) (Recall that there is a unique ring homomorphism from \(\Z\) to any other ring.) Since \(\phi(\Z)\) is a subring of \(K\text{,}\) it is an integral domain; since \(\Z/\ker\phi\cong \phi(\Z)\text{,}\) we see that \(\ker \phi\) is a prime ideal of \(\Z\text{.}\) It follows that \(\ker\phi=(0)\) or \(\ker\phi=(p)\text{,}\) where \(p\) is a prime integer.
    1. If \(\ker\phi=(0)\text{,}\) then \(\phi\) is injective, and thus \(m\cdot 1_K=\phi(m)\ne 0\) for all positive integers \(m\text{.}\) We see that \(\character K=0\) in this case.
      If \(\ker\phi=(p)\text{,}\) then \(p\) is the smallest positive element of \(\ker \phi\text{,}\) from which it follows that \(p\) is the smallest positive integer satisfying \(p\cdot 1_K=0\text{.}\) We see that \(\character K=p\) in this case.
    2. If \(\character K=0\text{,}\) then from the analysis above we see that \(\phi\) is injective. Using Theorem 1.1.9, since \(\Q=\Frac \Z\text{,}\) we see that \(\phi\) extends to an injective map \(\widetilde{\phi}\colon \Q\rightarrow K\text{,}\) defined as \(\widetilde{\phi}(m/n)=\phi(m)/\phi(n)=(m\cdot 1_K)/(n\cdot 1_K)\text{.}\) Its image
      \begin{equation} \widetilde{\phi}(\Q)=\{(m\cdot 1_K)/(n\cdot 1_K)\colon m,n\in \Z, n\ne 0\}\tag{1.4} \end{equation}
      is a subfield of \(K\) isomorphic to \(\Q\text{.}\) We claim that \(\widetilde{\phi}(\Q)\) is in fact the prime subfield of \(K\text{.}\) Indeed, any subfield \(K'\leq K\) must contain \(1_K\text{,}\) and hence \(m\cdot 1_K\) for all \(\in \Z\text{;}\) since \(K'\) also contains inverses of all nonzero elements, it also contains elements of the form \(1_K/(n\cdot 1_K)\text{,}\) \(n\ne 0\text{,}\) and hence all elements of the form \((m\cdot 1_K)/(n\cdot 1_K)\text{,}\) \(n\ne 0\text{.}\) It follows from (1.4) that \(\widetilde{\phi}(\Q)\leq K'\text{,}\) as desired.
    3. Assume \(\character K=p\text{.}\) In this case \(\ker \phi=(p)\) and
      \begin{equation*} \phi(\Z)\cong \Z/\ker\phi =\Z/p\Z=\F_p\text{.} \end{equation*}
      Thus \(\phi(\Z)\) is a subfield of \(K\) isomorphic to \(\F_p\text{.}\) It is easy to see that in fact \(\phi(\Z)\) is the prime subfield of \(K\text{,}\) since any subfield \(K'\) contains \(1_K\text{,}\) and hence also \(\{m\cdot 1_K\colon m\in \Z\}=\phi(Z)\text{.}\)

    Definition 1.2.6. Generated field.

    Given a field extension \(F\leq K\) and a subset \(A\subseteq K\) the field generated over \(F\) by \(A\text{,}\) denoted \(F(A)\text{,}\) is defined as
    \begin{equation*} F(A)=\bigcap_{\substack{F\leq K'\leq K\\ A\subseteq K'}}K'\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Equivalently, \(F(A)\) is the “smallest” subfield of \(K\) containing the set \(F\) and \(A\text{:}\) that is, if \(F\leq K'\leq K\) and \(A\subseteq K'\text{,}\) then \(F(A)\leq K'\text{.}\)
    When \(A=\{\alpha_1, \alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n\}\) (resp., \(A=\{\alpha_1, \alpha_2,\dots, \}\)), we denote \(F(A)\) as \(F(\alpha_1, \alpha_2,\dots, \alpha_n)\) (resp. \(F(\alpha_1, \alpha_2,\dots)\)).

    Procedure 1.2.7. Generated field.

    Given an extension \(F\leq K\) and subset \(A\subseteq K\text{,}\) to prove \(F(A)=L\text{,}\) proceed as follows:
    1. \(L\) contains \(F\) and \(A\).
      Show that \(L\) is a subfield of \(K\) containing \(F\) and \(A\text{.}\)
    2. \(L\) is the smallest such field.
      Show that for any subfield \(K'\) of \(K\) containing \(F\) and \(A\text{,}\) we have \(L\subseteq K'\text{.}\)

    Example 1.2.8. Field generated by element.

    Consider the extension \(\R/\Q\text{.}\) Show that \(\Q(\sqrt{2})=\{a+b\sqrt{2}\colon a,b\in \Q\}\text{.}\)
    Solution.
    Let \(L=\{a+b\sqrt{2}\colon a,b\in \Q\}\text{.}\) We follow Procedure 1.2.7 to show \(\Q(\sqrt{2})=L\text{.}\)
    1. We have seen earlier that \(L\) is a subfield of \(\R\text{,}\) and clearly it contains \(\Q\) and \(\sqrt{2}\text{.}\)
    2. Suppose \(K'\) is a subfield of \(\R\) containing \(\Q\) and \(\sqrt{2}\text{.}\) We must show that \(L\subseteq K'\text{.}\) Take any \(a+b\sqrt{2}\in L\text{.}\) Since by definition \(a,b\in \Q\text{,}\) and since by assumption \(K'\) contains \(\Q\) and \(\sqrt{2}\text{,}\) we have \(a,b,\sqrt{2}\in K'\text{.}\) Since \(K'\) is a subring of \(\R\text{,}\) and hence closed under addition and multiplication, we have \(a+b\sqrt{2}\in K'\text{,}\) as desired.
